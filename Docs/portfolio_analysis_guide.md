# Product Manager Portfolio - Section Guidelines & Best Practices

*Based on analysis of successful PM portfolios*

## Core Section Framework

### 1. **Hero Section** (Above the fold)
**Purpose:** First impression and value proposition

**Guidelines:**
- **Keep it concise:** 1-2 sentences max that immediately communicate your value
- **Lead with impact:** Focus on outcomes you drive, not just what you do
- **Make it memorable:** Use specific, compelling language that differentiates you
- **Include a professional photo:** Builds trust and connection

**Examples from analyzed portfolios:**
- Mark Progano: Leads with testimonial quote emphasizing technical prowess and problem-solving
- Surabhi: Personal, authentic approach: "engineer learning how to build great products"
- Simon Pan: Immediately showcases specific metrics from major companies

**Best Practice:** Lead with your strongest differentiator - whether it's specific metrics, company credibility, or unique background.

---

### 2. **Featured Work/Case Studies** (Primary content)
**Purpose:** Demonstrate impact through specific examples

**Guidelines:**
- **Lead with results:** Start each case study with the key metric or outcome
- **Use the CAR format:** Challenge, Approach, Results
- **Include visuals:** Screenshots, prototypes, or process diagrams
- **Quantify everything:** Specific percentages, dollar amounts, user counts
- **Tell the story:** Don't just list features; explain the problem-solving journey
- **Keep it scannable:** Use headers, bullet points, and white space

**Structure Pattern:**
```
[Product Name] | [Company] | [Timeframe]
[One-line impact statement with key metric]
- Challenge: What problem were you solving?
- Approach: How did you solve it? (methodology, process)
- Results: What was the measurable impact?
[Optional: Lessons learned or what you'd do differently]
```

**Best Practice:** Lead with 2-3 of your strongest case studies. Each should demonstrate different skills (growth, 0→1, turnaround, scale, etc.)

---

### 3. **Skills & Expertise** (Supporting content)
**Purpose:** Demonstrate breadth and depth of capabilities

**Guidelines:**
- **Organize by relevance:** Put most important skills first
- **Be specific:** Instead of "Analytics," say "A/B testing, cohort analysis, funnel optimization"
- **Group logically:** Functional skills, technical skills, industry experience
- **Use progressive disclosure:** High-level categories with specific details on hover/click
- **Include proficiency levels:** Beginner, Intermediate, Expert (when appropriate)

**Categories observed:**
- **Product Strategy:** Roadmapping, prioritization, market analysis
- **Technical:** APIs, databases, ML/AI, specific platforms
- **Leadership:** Team management, cross-functional collaboration, mentorship
- **Growth:** User acquisition, retention, experimentation
- **Industry:** B2B, B2C, specific verticals

**Best Practice:** Tailor skill emphasis to target roles. Senior roles should emphasize strategy and leadership over tactical skills.

---

### 4. **Professional Timeline/Experience** (Credibility)
**Purpose:** Establish career progression and company credibility

**Guidelines:**
- **Show progression:** Highlight promotions and increasing responsibility
- **Company recognition:** Include well-known companies prominently
- **Brief descriptions:** 1-2 lines per role focusing on scope and impact
- **Consistent format:** Company, title, dates, key achievement
- **Visual timeline:** Consider graphic representation for easy scanning

**Format:**
```
[Title] | [Company] | [Dates]
Key impact statement or scope of responsibility
```

**Best Practice:** Lead with most impressive/recent roles. For director-level, emphasize team size, budget, and strategic impact.

---

### 5. **Personal Touch/Personality** (Connection)
**Purpose:** Show personality and cultural fit

**Guidelines:**
- **Be authentic:** Share genuine interests and perspectives
- **Keep it professional:** Relevant to work context
- **Show depth:** Demonstrate thinking beyond just work
- **Include variety:** Mix of professional and personal interests
- **Use visuals:** Photos from speaking, hobbies, travel

**Examples from portfolios:**
- Surabhi: "building sustainable livelihoods for farmers" + art + cycling
- Visual elements showing personality and interests beyond work

**Best Practice:** Include 2-3 elements that make you memorable and demonstrate cultural fit.

---

### 6. **Social Proof/Testimonials** (Credibility)
**Purpose:** Third-party validation of your impact

**Guidelines:**
- **Use specific quotes:** Avoid generic praise; focus on specific skills/impact
- **Include attribution:** Name, title, company, relationship to you
- **Vary sources:** Managers, peers, direct reports, cross-functional partners
- **Keep it recent:** Within 2-3 years for maximum relevance
- **Strategic placement:** Throughout the site, not just one section

**Best Practice:** 3-5 strong testimonials that reinforce your key value propositions.

---

### 7. **Contact/Next Steps** (Call to action)
**Purpose:** Make it easy for people to reach out

**Guidelines:**
- **Multiple channels:** Email, LinkedIn, calendar link
- **Clear availability:** Current status (actively looking, open to opportunities, etc.)
- **Response expectations:** How quickly you typically respond
- **What you're looking for:** Types of opportunities or conversations you want

**Best Practice:** Make it ridiculously easy for the right people to connect with you.

## Design & Technical Guidelines

### **Information Hierarchy**
1. **Above the fold:** Who you are + key value proposition
2. **Primary content:** 2-3 strongest case studies with metrics
3. **Supporting content:** Skills, experience, testimonials
4. **Personal connection:** Personality, interests, thought leadership
5. **Call to action:** Contact information and next steps

### **Content Principles**
- **Specificity over generality:** "Increased retention 40%" not "improved user experience"
- **Impact over process:** Lead with results, then explain how you got there
- **Story over resume:** Show problem-solving journey, not just job history
- **Evidence over claims:** Metrics, testimonials, visuals that prove your impact

### **Visual Design**
- **Clean and scannable:** Lots of white space, clear hierarchy
- **Professional but personal:** Reflects your personality while maintaining credibility
- **Mobile-first:** Ensure all content works well on mobile devices
- **Fast loading:** Optimize images and minimize heavy elements

### **Common Pitfalls to Avoid**
- **Too much text:** Walls of text that nobody reads
- **Generic language:** Could apply to any product manager
- **Process over impact:** Focusing on methodology instead of results
- **Outdated examples:** Case studies more than 3-4 years old
- **Hard to contact:** Buried or missing contact information

## Adaptation for Your Level (Director+)

### **Emphasize Strategic Impact**
- Focus on business outcomes, not feature delivery
- Highlight team and organizational development
- Show market-level thinking and competitive positioning

### **Demonstrate Leadership**
- Include examples of building/scaling teams
- Show cross-functional influence and stakeholder management
- Highlight mentorship and organizational impact

### **Industry Recognition**
- Speaking engagements, thought leadership, patents
- Awards, recognition, industry involvement
- Board positions, advisory roles, consulting

### **Scale and Scope**
- Budget responsibility, P&L ownership
- Market size, user scale, revenue impact
- Geographic or multi-product portfolio management
